About

I am a PhD student in economics at the Paris School of Economics, supervised by Mathieu Parenti. My research focuses on the economic and environmental consequences of globalization, with particular interests in unilateral climate policy, innovation dynamics, and international technology diffusion.

I am a research assistant at the Institute for Macroeconomic and International Policies at the Paris School of Economics, working on the New Quantitative Models of International Trade programme. I am also a replicator for The Economic Journal and The Econometrics Journal.

Research

Cap-and-Trade in the Open Economy: Innovation, Exports, and the Cost of Decarbonization.
Work in progress. With Mathieu Parenti, Riccardo Norbiato, and Noemi Walczak.
